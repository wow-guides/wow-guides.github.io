Introduction

Do you like tank healing? Are you a fan of very high single target heals? If so, this is the build for you. Holy paladin absolutely crushes the charts with large heals that are often doubled. What it lacks in aoe healing, the Holy Paladin makes up for in single target healing and brings loads of buffs to boot.

You will seldom see a raid without a Holy Paladin and for good reason.

Build

Talents:

There are two major talent strains you want to consider. One that focuses on raid utility and another which focuses on healing output.

Conviction (51/5/15) Divine Sacrifice (51/5/15)

Glyphs:

Now, the choice is up to you. Are you a Flash of Light person, focusing on casting super quick, super efficient heals? Or do you wish to dable in massive heals at a bit of a slower pace. This choice will determine which primary glyph you want.

Use Glyph of Seal of Light if you are focusing on Flash of Light

Use Glyph of Seal of Wisdom if you are focusing on Holy Light

In either case, there are a few other viable glyphs but I highly recommend Holy Light for the minor AoE healing. Even if you're focusing Flash of Light, it's good to use here and there to help with the melee. I also recommend Beacon of Light for the increased duration.

Buffs:

You are the Queen of Buffs and you will be expected to give every asshole you're carrying whatever different buff they desire simply because it'll make them feel like a big boy. Don't let that discourage you, though.

  1. (Greater) Blessing of Might

  5. Retribution Aura
  6. Concentration Aura
  7. Fire Resistance Aura
  8. Frost Resistance Aura
  9. Shadow Resistance Aura
  10. Crusader Aura

Greater Blessings can be applied to every one of a particular class in range. If you've got multiple Paladins you should coordinate which one is giving which buff to which class. This can be confusing but don't worry, some asshole Rogue or Warrior will tell you when you've fucked up this complicated process. Furthermore, Auras are selected using a stance bar. Each Paladin can give an aura and the auras do not overlap. Coordinate with the other paladins but your default aura should always be Concentration. Unless you want to piss off the group then roll Crusader Aura.

Single Target Healing:

Throw Sacred shield on the tank, Beacon of Light on the tank OR a second target you want to get heals, and spam Flash of Light or Holy Light on the tank, using Holy Shock on cooldown or as needed. For dungeon groups I prefer to beacon the tank and heal the members as I have to handle party healing as well. For raid content I prefer to directly heal the main tank and beacon the off tank. The reason for this is because it's easier to keep up the heal over time effect of using Flash of Light on a target with Sacred Shield. Lastly, always remember to use Judgement of Light on the boss or one of the adds. The passive healing from the judgement is massive, especially in a raid.

Multi Target Healing:

This can be slightly more complicated. Throw Sacred shield on the tank, Beacon of Light on the tank OR a second target you want to get heals, and spam Flash of Light or Holy Light on party members, using Holy Shock on cooldown or as needed. Apart from the Glyph of Holy Light and Judgement of Light, there isn't a whole hell of a lot you can do in terms of AOE healing, so it's essentially taking the single target healing skills and spamming them on multiple allies. For this reason hover over macros and addons like Clique and Healbot go a long way.

Seals:

As Above. This is based on what spell you're making your main focus and the seal you've glyphed for.

Flash of Light:
Seal of Light
Holy Light:
Seal of Wisdom

Cooldown Use:


Avenging Wrath
• As much as possible. It's a massive healing increase you can't afford not to use.
Hammer of Justice
• Great to stun casting adds.
Divine Favor
• Guaranteed Critical on next Flash of Light, Holy Light, and Holy Shock. Great in a pinch.
Divine Illumination
• Reduces the mana cost of all spells by 50%. Great for burst damage phases and in conjuction with Avenging Wrath.
Divine Plea
• Returns mana based percentage of total mana pool but reduces healing by 50%. Use below 80% and on cooldown thereafter provided you're not in a burst phase.
Hand of Protection
• This is a massive dps loss but great in a pinch to save yourself and others from attacking adds and standing in the bad.
Hand of Salvation
• Great way to scrub off some threat should that be an issue.
Hand of Freedom
• Negates control effects. Incredibly useful against slows.
Divine Shield
• The ultimate bubble. If the raid is about to wipe, pop this and hearth.
Aura Mastery
• Great in a pinch for increase the effectiveness of some auras. It's not that great with Retribution Aura or Crusader Aura but amazing with the others.
Divine Intervention
• Use this on the tank as they're about to pull. Or, more optimally, use it on another healer as the group is about to wipe. It sacrifices you but makes them invincible.

Gearing

Flash of Light:

Holy Paladins focused on casting Flash of Light should do whatever they can to increase the healing of that spell. Stack spellpower, befriend an elemental shaman, pray to RNGesus for good crits. Anything you can do to increase the specific healing output of that spell will make you a better healer. Do these dark deeds and you will be rewarded with very efficient, very quick heals.

Holy Light:

Holy Paladins focused on the spell Holy Light should do anything in their power to increase their mana pool. Stack int, befriend a mage, pray to the old gods, all of it could be instrumental in keeping you afloat as a healer. If you manage to procure a large enough mana pool you are rewarded by having the greatest potential healing output in the game.


Simplified:
Intellect ≥ Spell Power > Crit > Mp5 > Haste

Itemization:

You'll want to focus on getting plate gear with Intellect and Mp5. For rings and trinkets, you could use gear that has intellect and spirit but that is suboptimal.


Gemming:

You've got a choice. Either Brilliant King's Amber OR Runed Cardinal Ruby based on whether you're going Holy Light (Brilliant) or Flash of Light (Runed)

Introduction

The bane of competent raid members everywhere, the faceroller will stumble through the hardest content and manage to get top damage on nearly every fight, save for the ones where they pull aggro and die.

Yes! This is the guide you've been looking for, even if you don't quite know it yet. This guide will teach you the basics of how to roll your knuckles across your keyboard and make holy damage happen. You will learn things such as, Divine Storm spam, dropping Consecration on everything, and forgetting to turn off Righteous Fury.

Pay attention, or don't because it doesn't matter, and you will become the best Ret paladin too.

Doing Damage:

I was thinking of creating two sections, one discussing single target and one regarding multiple target. Then I realized they were the same rotation.

Simplified:
Hit every button on cooldown.
Complex:
Judgement > Hammer of Vengeance > Divine Storm ≥ Exorcism > Crusader Strike > Consecration (mana permitting)

For single target without the T10 2 piece bonus, I'd put Exorcism over Divine Storm. Otherwise, Divine Storm your face off. Consecration should only be considered if you've got the mana. If you're running low, skip it for 10-15 seconds or so. If you keep judgement high in the priority, you should have mana to spare.

Gearing

Damage:

Get hit capped as quick as you can. The melee hit cap is 8%. Once you've reached the hit cap, focus on getting enough expertise. 16 should do it because you will get 10 expertise from using the Glyph of Seal of Vengeance. Beyond this focus on strength and crit. Haste is mediocre and while Armor Penetration increases the damage of your melee strikes and Seal of Vengeance/Corruption, it does not effect any other spells.


Simplified:
264 Hit Rating (8%) > 16 Expertise > Strength > Critical Strike > Haste > Armor Penetration

Itemization:

You'll want to focus on getting plate gear with strength and crit on it. Try to avoid grabbing obvious tanking pieces and bits with Armor Penetration on them unless they are vastly superior to the item you currently have. Furthermore, you'll want a slow two handed weapon to compliment your array of hard hitting spells.


Gemming:

I typically follow the socket requirements because "free stats", but most of your options are extremely limited. Thus I recommend putting a single Nightmare Tear in a blue socket and filling all other blue sockets with Cardinal Rubies.
