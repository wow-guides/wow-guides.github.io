Beastmastery
Written by: FluffyPira


Contents
  1. Disclaimer
  2. Introduction
  3. Build
  4. Spells
  5. Gearing
  6. Macros

Disclaimer

While Beastmastery hunter can be a great build for sole pve content and world exploration, it is not recommended as a pve build. The damage is simply too low to be considered viable in a raid, doing 67% of the damage a Marksman hunter can do in the same gear. That said, if you still wish to play beastmastery because of preference or desire to experiment, there is nothing stopping you.


Introduction

Beastmastery is a unique hunter build that focuses largely on buffing the hunter's pet to deal damage. As such it is much more dependant on the hunter's pet being survivable and dealing the most damage it can.

By the end of this guide you should be able to understand the basics of playing a Beastmastery Hunter.

Build

Talent:

This is your standard MM hunter talent setup, everything you need to succeed is right here. The only modification is when you get to the point where arcane shot is a dps loss, you will want to move those talent points somewhere else.

Standard Build (52/14/5)

Glyphs:

Major:

  • Glyph of Bestial Wrath
  • Glyph of Steady Shot
  • Glyph of the Hawk
Minor:
  • Fairly unimportant. Take whatever

Pets:

For pets, you've basically two options.

  1. Devilsaur: Highest pet dps in the game; only available to Beastmastery Hunters.
  2. Wolf: Very good option due to the Furious Howl buff.

Spells

Hunters use quite a few abilities, but it shouldn't be too hard to keep up with because there aren't any surprise procs in this build.

Damage:
Kill Shot (KS):
Execution ability, does massive damage if your target is below 20% hp, this is what gives you a dps spike at the end of fights.
Serpent Sting (SS):
You will always open with this, and it should always stay on your target for the entirety of any fight.
Arcane Shot:
This is about the only shot you will be using during the fight apart from steady shot. Use this as much as possible.
Steady Shot:
This is your filler. Any time your other attacks are on cooldown, fill with this.
Kill Command:
This attack is not tied to your global cooldown, so it should be macroed onto your shots.
Cooldowns:
Hunter's Mark:
Free damage. If this isn't up at all times, then you are playing hunter wrong.
Rapid Fire (RF):
Attack speed buff, very useful for heavy burst.
Misdirection:
This transfers your aggro to someone else of your choosing for the next 4 seconds. Can be used to help your tank if they are having aggro issues.
Aspect of the (Dragon)Hawk:
This is your dps aspect, keep it on during all boss fights.
Aspect of the Viper:
If you run out of mana switch to this temporarily. Your dps will drop, but you will quickly regain mana. Switch back immediately to Hawk/Dragonhawk when your mana is at a sufficient amount.

Single Target

Always apply Hunter's Mark ahead of time before the fight starts. Also, to help with the pull try to apply misdirection to the main tank of the fight as the fight starts. Beyond this, beastmastery follows a very simple priority

Single Target:
KS > SS > Arcane Shot > Steady Shot

Multi Target

Not much to say here, standard priority but replace aimed with multi-shot. Volley for 4+ targets.

Simplified:
Volley & Multi-Shot

Gearing

Damage:

Get hit capped. After that you'll want to focus on agi and crit as much as possible.


Simplified:
Hit Cap (8%) > Crit > Haste > Armor Penetration

Itemization:

You'll want to find either a single two hand weapon with agility on it or two one handed weapons. Ranged weapon however is the most imporant thing to worry about. Try to chase after gear you think enhancement shaman would love. Crit/Haste while trying to avoid Armor Penetration, although some Arpen isn't bad.


Gemming:

You'll want to focus on maximizing agility here for attack power and crit reasons.


Meta:
Relentless Earthsiege Diamond
Red:
Delicate Cardinal Ruby
Yellow:
At least one Nightmare Tear in yellow or blue. Delicate Cardinal Ruby
Blue:
At least one Nightmare Tear in yellow or blue. Delicate Cardinal Ruby

Useful Macros

Misdirection

#showtooltip
/cast [@focus,help][help][@pet,exists] Misdirection

Steady Shot

#showtooltip
/cast Steady Shot
/cast ![target=pettarget,exists] Kill Command
/script UIErrorsFrame:Clear()


Marksmanship
Written by: Fiery


Contents
  1. Introduction
  2. Build
  3. Spells
  4. Gearing

Introduction

Marksmanship Hunter is one of the highest dps specializations in the game. They provide heavy physical damage from a safe range, can help with pulling certain mobs, and can help with tanks who are having an issues building aggro.

By the end of this guide you should be able to understand the basics of playing a Marksman Hunter, so you can provide both excellent dps and strong utility for your raids and dungeons.

Build

Talent:

This is your standard MM hunter talent setup, everything you need to succeed is right here. The only modification is when you get to the point where arcane shot is a dps loss, you will want to move those talent points somewhere else.

Standard Build (7/57/7)

Glyphs:

Major:

  • Glyph of Serpent Sting
  • Glyph of Kill Shot
  • Glyph of Chimera Shot
Minor:
  • Fairly unimportant. Take whatever

Pets:

For pets, if you want to be serious about it, you only have really 4 options.

  1. Wolf: Best overall option due to the Furious Howl buff.
  2. Wasp: Good dps, and an armor debuff if you don't have anyone with faerie fire in your party.
  3. Raptor: A decent option for DPS.
  4. Cat: Almost as good as raptor

Spells

Hunters use quite a few abilities, but it shouldn't be too hard to keep up with because there aren't any surprise procs in this build.

Damage:
Kill Shot (KS):
Execution ability, does massive damage if your target is below 20% hp, this is what gives you a dps spike at the end of fights.
Serpent Sting (SS):
You will always open with this, and it should always stay on your target for the entirety of any fight.
Chimera Shot:
Will refresh your serpent sting and deal a lot of damage.
Aimed Shot:
Decreases target's healing recieved by 50%, but that's not important to us, its just another attack to help us pump out more damage.
Arcane Shot:
Because this spell uses arcane damage, there is actually a point where if you have enough armor penetration using this will become a dps loss. Test it from time to time to make sure it is still good to use for you.
Steady Shot:
This is your filler. Any time your other attacks are on cooldown, fill with this.
Silencing Shot:
This attack is not tied to your global cooldown, so it will be macroed onto all of your attacks so it can be cast ASAP. An example will be provided in the macro section.
Cooldowns:
Hunter's Mark:
Free damage. If this isn't up at all times, then you are playing hunter wrong.
Rapid Fire (RF):
Attack speed buff, very useful for heavy burst.
Readiness:
Resets the cooldown on every skill.
Misdirection:
This transfers your aggro to someone else of your choosing for the next 4 seconds. Can be used to help your tank if they are having aggro issues.
Trueshot Aura:
Raidwide attack power buff. Make sure it's always on.
Aspect of the (Dragon)Hawk:
This is your dps aspect, keep it on during all boss fights.
Aspect of the Viper:
If you run out of mana switch to this temporarily. Your dps will drop, but you will quickly regain mana. Switch back immediately to Hawk/Dragonhawk when your mana is at a sufficient amount.

Single Target

Always apply Hunter's Mark ahead of time before the fight starts. After that opening rotation, you will go to your standard priority. Other than that the only other thing you need to know is how to pull targets for your tank. Note, do not do this unless you already have permission from your tank, or it will probably piss them off and get you kicked from your raid.

To help a tank pull a mob you will want to apply misdirection on them and then attack the mob with a ranged attack so they will receive the aggro without having to get close to the target and risk pulling extra enemies. There are macros that can help you with this and I will include one in the macro section.

Opener:
SS -> RF -> Chimera -> Aimed -> Arcane -> Readiness ->
Chimera Shot -> Arcane Shot -> Rapid Fire (when first falls off)

Simplified:
KS > SS > Chimera > Aimed > Arcane > Steady

Multi Target

Not much to say here, standard priority but replace aimed with multi-shot. Volley for 4+ targets.

Simplified:
Volley & Multi-Shot

Gearing

Damage:

The first thing you need to do, absolutely, is to get hit capped. After that it's all Armor Penetration, all the way.


Simplified:
Hit Cap > Armor Penetration Cap > Crit > Haste

Itemization:

You'll want to find either a single two hand weapon with agility on it or two one handed weapons. Ranged weapon however is the most imporant thing to worry about.


Gemming:

Do not gem armor penetration unless you already have at least 50% armor penetration from your gear, otherwise ALWAYS gem strength. Armor Penetration scales exponentially, meaning the more you have the higher the stat weight. It becomes better than Strength at about 50%.


Meta:
Relentless Earthsiege Diamond
Red:
Fractured Cardinal Ruby, or Delicate Cardinal Ruby
Yellow:
At least one Nightmare Tear in yellow or blue. Fractured Cardinal Ruby, or Delicate Cardinal Ruby
Blue:
At least one Nightmare Tear in yellow or blue. Fractured Cardinal Ruby, or Delicate Cardinal Ruby

Useful Macros

Misdirection

Pulling a mob for the tank: Be sure for this you have your focus set on the tank, to do so, right click them in the raid page, and click "set focus." 
#showtooltip Misdirection
/cast [target=focus] Misdirection

Silencing Shot

This is how you macro silencing shot into your spells. 
#showtooltip Steady Shot
/cast Silencing Shot
/cast Steady Shot


Survival
Written by: FluffyPira

Introduction

If you're looking for high dps, you've come to the wrong place. You'll want to look up the guide for Marksmanship Hunter. If the prospect of crap damage hasn't purturbed you, you're WAY into the mechanics of explosive shot, or you're lookng to settle a bet with some Fury warrior who is like "omg that spec isn't viable," you've come to the right place.

Welcome to Survival Hunter, where the DPS is crap but the mechanics are super fun. Here you will find all the information you need to succeed as best as you can given the limitations of the spec. You will never be the highest dps, even in the same gear MM hunters will outpace you. Get used to that, you're not here to worry about top dps but to enjoy playing a spec you like.

Build

Talent:

This is your standard talent setup. There's a bit of leeway in points, as you can see many talents are left unfilled or fillde partially, but whenever I look for a spec this is what I find. You do want at least 11 if not the full 15 into marksmanship because of Aimed Shot.

Standard Build (3/15/53) Alternate Build (2/18/51)

Glyphs:

Major:

  • Glyph of Explosive Shot
  • Glyph of Kill Shot
  • Glyph of Aimed Shot
Minor:
  • Fairly unimportant. Take whatever

Pets:

For pets, if you want to be serious about it, you only have really 4 options.

  1. Wolf: Best overall option due to the Furious Howl buff.
  2. Wasp: Good dps, and an armor debuff if you don't have anyone with faerie fire in your party.
  3. Raptor: A decent option for DPS.
  4. Cat: Almost as good as raptor

Spells

Damage:
  • Explosive Shot
  • Kill Shot
  • Serpent Sting
  • Black Arrow
  • Steady Shot
  • Aimed Shot
  • Kill Command
Cooldowns:
Hunter's Mark:
Keep this mark on the target at all times. If you have another hunter, talk to them before hand and see whose responsibility it is.
Rapid Fire (RF):
Personal attack speed buff. Use it 5-10 seconds into a fight when your trinkets proc and shmana use BL/Hero.
Misdirection:
This skill transfers aggro, use it with a macro to make it easy and more effective.
Aspect of the Dragonhawk:
This is your main aspect. Keep it up as much as you can unless you're critically low on mana than switch to...
Aspect of the Viper:
This aspect is when you run out of mana. You will deal considerably less damage but regain mana. Switch back once you think you have enough.

Single Target

Survival has a few procs, and I've read guides that say the rotation can be "hard." I've never really seen that as the case. The only important thing to note really is to keep Serpent Sting up at all times if possible and when Lock and Load procs, use Explosive shot and wait a sec for the first one to clear before firing a second.

When other shots are on cooldown, always fill in with Steady Shot. Macro Kill Command to Steady Shot so it will cast as you spam.

Simplified
Serpent Sting > Kill > Explosive > Black Arrow > Aimed Shot

Multi Target

Not much to say here, standard priority but replace aimed with multi-shot. Volley for 4+ targets.

Simplified:
Volley & Multi-Shot

Gearing

Damage:

Get hit capped. After that you'll want to focus on agi and crit as much as possible. Most of your damage should come by way of explosive shot, so armor penetration is realtively useless compared to crit and haste.


Simplified:
Hit Cap (8%) > Crit > Haste > Armor Penetration

Itemization:

You'll want to find either a single two hand weapon with agility on it or two one handed weapons. Ranged weapon however is the most imporant thing to worry about. Try to chase after gear you think enhancement shaman would love. Crit/Haste while trying to avoid Armor Penetration, although some Arpen isn't bad.


Gemming:

You'll want to focus on maximizing agility here for attack power and crit reasons.


Meta:
Relentless Earthsiege Diamond
Red:
Delicate Cardinal Ruby
Yellow:
At least one Nightmare Tear in yellow or blue. Delicate Cardinal Ruby
Blue:
At least one Nightmare Tear in yellow or blue. Delicate Cardinal Ruby
