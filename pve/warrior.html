Introduction

Fury warriors are a very easy class that, somehow, many people dont understand how to play. Luckily you will not have that problem by the end of this guide, as I will attempt to cover everything from what kind of gear you want, to what talents to use, and what kind of macros you need to make your life easier.

They have the highest potential of all dps classes in 3.3.5 in terms of boss damage, and does excellent multi-target damage as well. They have really good survivability with plate armor, so fight mechanics can be especially forgiving. I personally see almost no downside to fury warriors, except maybe the rotation can get a little boring after a while. There are ways to spice it up though... if you're into (stance) dancing that is.

Rotation

This is the most simple rotation you will ever have to remember. There is only one variable in it, and that is heroic strike/cleave. You do not want to spam strike/cleave unless you have excess rage(> 50 rage). When you first start gearing your rage generation will be terrible, so you won't want to use the macros I will provide further down until you get better gear and start playing raids like ICC.

WW > BT> Slam(on proc), use heroic strike as often as you can without sacrificing so much rage that you can't use the first 3 abilities. Use cleave instead of hs in AOE situations.

If you are stance dancing, I have left a macro at the end of the guide. At lower gear stance dancing is not worth it and you will waste more dps than you gain by trying to do it. Be sure to test it beforehand and make sure you are efficient enough to do it.

Rage dump with Heroic Strike for single target and Cleave for multi target when available.


Simplified:
WW > BT > Execute > Slam (proc) > Rend

Cooldown Use


Battle Shout
Your primary buff, increases attack power.
Commanding Shout
Only use this if someone else is using battle shout or if you have a Paladin in your group using Blessing of Might.
Berserker Rage
You can use them to help generate rage in situationg where bosses do a decent amount of AOE damage. Can also be used to negate some boss effects such as Blood Queen's fear.
Death Wish
This is your main dps cooldown, when everyone pops cooldowns, you pop this. 20% Damage increase is fun and is best used with heroism/bloodlust.
Recklesness
Use this whenever, shouldn't matter because you should have plenty of time to get all 3 special hits in before the buff wears off no matter what.
Bloodrage
Spam this everytime it's up, you need all the help you can get with rage generation, it's used automatically in my macros I give you in this guide.

Gearing

Damage:

The Expertise cap is 26, Hit cap is 8% but becuase of our talent, Precision, you will only need 5%. The Armor Penetration cap is 1400 (100%). These are the most important stats to consider for a fury warrior.


Simplified:
Hit Cap> Expertise Cap > Armor Penetration Cap > Crit > Haste

Itemization:

Use a two-handed weapon in each hand once you have Titan's Grip. Keep to two handers with strength on them, polearms will not work.

Do not gem armor penetration unless you already have at least 50% armor penetration from your gear, otherwise ALWAYS gem strength. Armor Penetration scales exponentially, meaning the more you have the higher the stat weight. It becomes better than Strength at about 50%.

Introduction

Welcome to the world of Warrior tanking. You are the archetypal tank. When people think "tank", they're thinking of you. You don't need no silly magic or holy power to make mobs hate you. You just roll out of bed with a shield in one hand and a sword in the other, ready to get smacked around by a raid boss. Don't forget to bring a healer though...

The main draw to a warrior tank is superior damage reduction through blocking, as well as increased raid damage by way of sunder/devastate. The warrior tank is very player centric and doesn't bring much in the way of party buffs. What the warrior does bring though is superior control, survivability, and threat. What a warrior lacks in party utility, it makes up for in its vast array of cooldowns and situational attacks.

Build

Talent:

Warrior tanks have some limited choice in terms of building a talent tree. At least 51 points must be spent in the Protection tree, which leaves 20 to play around with.

There's really only two competing builds. One goes further into arms and picks up Deep Wounds for increase threat. The other picks up Imrpoved Disciplines and improved Demoralising Shout. The Deep Wounds build is therefore the standard and will work regardless of gear level. When you get around 5500 gearscore, and want to change your focus to pure survivability for heroic encounters and the like, that option is available as well.

Single Target

For single target, the most important thing you can do is Shield Slam. It will do high threat and increase your block. Beyond that, use Revenge on cooldown and Devastate to fill in the gaps. If you use Rend, you can also apply that when not using Shield Slam and Revenge. Pay mind to Sword and Board procs and use Shield Slam ASAP.

Try to keep around 40 rage in your pool. If you find yourself reaching 100, feel free to dump with heroic strike.


Simplified:
SS > Rev > Rend (optional) > Deva

Multi Target

For multi-target pulls charge in and immediately lay down a Thunder Clap. Round up the mobs into a tight little ball in front of you and use Shockwave. While Thunder Clap and Shockwave are on cooldown, tab target the mobs making sure you have sufficient threat, Slamming, using Revenge, and Devastating the lowest threat target as filler.

Try to keep around 40 rage in your pool. If you find yourself reaching 100, feel free to dump with cleave.


Simplified:
SW > TC > Rev > SS > Deva

Cooldown Use


Shield Wall
This is your largest source of damage reduction. Use this as an "oh shit button" when you notice your health dropping rapidly, your healer is out of mana, or your healer is completely lost.
Shield Block
This increases your block 100% and only has a 40s cooldown for 10 seconds of uptime. Use it to start a fight and on cooldown, as it's not only mitigates a tonne of damage it also builds rage.
Last Stand
Increases your health by 30% of maxiumum health. Great cooldown for times when you drop below 50% and need a bit of a health boost. Does nothing to reduce damage so be aware.
Spell Reflect
This cooldown does exactly what it says it will do and reflects a single spell back to the caster. The spell must be reflected in the 5 second window before the buff disappears.
Bloodrage
This spell trades health for rage. With a minor glyph, health requirement can be removed. With 4pT10 bonus it becomes a damage absorbtion bubble good for 20% of the user's max health.
Concussion Blow
Deals damage and stuns a target.
Intervene
Charges at a friendly target and intercepts the next attack against them. Also reduces their threat 10%
Berserker Rage
Prevents the user from being affected by fear, sap, and incapacitate effects. Also provides a healthy amount of rage when hit.

So when do I shout?

I'm getting to that. As the protection warrior, you will want to shout Commanding Shout as your buff shout. Use and maintain Demoralizing Shout during a pull. Challenging Shout is an AOE taunt that's great for wrangling in stray mobs. Intimidating Shout should only be used in situations where you've overwhelmed by AOE. Battle Shout if you are with a Warlock who uses an imp pet or tanking alongside another protection warrior.

Gearing

Threat:

If you worry about your threat, the best thing you can do is get hit and expertise capped. The expertise cap is 26 for dodge and 56 exprtise for parry. Although you'll be tanking, the dodge cap is enough. Hit cap is 8%.


Simplified:
263 hit rating > 200 Expertise rating > *

Defense:

Most importantly, above all else, is to get defense capped. If you're not at the cap, there is a chance that raid bosses will critically strike you, dealing bonkers amounts of damage to you and scaring the healers. To prevent this you MUST reach a defense of 540. Beyond this, you'll be looking for stamina primarily followed by parry and dodge. Dodge rating will result in slightly better returns than parry, however, as both have a diminising return it's best not to go too hard in a single direction.


Simplified:
540 Defense > Stamina > Dodge = Parry > Defense Rating*
* = Beyond 540 defense

Itemization:

This is a very sword and board kind of class and thus you will want a tanking one handed axe, sword, or mace in your main hand and a tanking shield in your off hand. High level and more aggressive Prot Warriors should feel free to use a DPS one handed weapon, provided they don't lose defense cap.

Beyond weapon, every single item on your character should be a plate tanking item. These items should have some combination of Strength, Stamina, Bonus Armor, Defense, Dodge, Parry, Hit, and Expertise. You will never see Armor Penetration, Crit, or Haste on tank armor, jewelery, or trinkets. Unless you are extremely experienced and know the risks associated with running dps and pvp gear, please stick to tanking gear only.
