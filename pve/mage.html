Introduction

Are you someone who loves to gamble? Well this one's for you. Welcome to the world of Deep Fire, where every single fight is a question of whether or not you will get more or less crit returns than your crit rating. Spoiler: It's often less.

Fire mage, for all intents and purposes, is a DoT class. You will deal long slow Fireballs and proc instant Pyroblasts, but you will deal a significant amount of damage with Incite, Living Bomb, and Pyroblast damage-over-time effects.

Build

Talent:

Here is where you have to make the tough choice, because there's really only 2 viable DPS builds. So think to yourself: 

Do I like Frostfire Bolt better than Fireball?

In truth, both are viable and both are suited to different situations. Since FB/TTW is more dependant on hit and slightly more mana intensive, I'd recommend fresh 80s go with FFB to start. You'll be doing the same core things regardless of spec, but using a different filler spell. That said, there are individual spell changes to pay attention. Fireball specific elements of the guide will be marked (FB/TTW Only), Frostfire specific elements of the guide will be marked (FFB Only).

Single Target

Living Bomb! Let it fall off but refresh it as quickly as you can afterward. When it falls off it goes "boom" and will often net you a free crit. If you're only person in the raid capable for providing the increase crit debuff to the boss, Improved Scorch should be your next focus. Refresh it before it falls off. After that, Pyroblast on hot streaks. It's a free pyroblast, it's important, it should be highly prioritized. Fill in the rest of the damage with Fireball (FB/TTW) OR Frostfire Bolt (FFB).


Simplified:
FB/TTW 
Living Bomb > Improved Scorch > Pyroblast! > Fireball

FFB 
Living Bomb > Improved Scorch > Pyroblast! > Frostfire Bolt

Multi Target

For AoE, the best damage you can do is probably by casting Living Bomb on all the things and watch the tank scream at you in raid or party chat. If you truly hate your tank, after applying like 6 Living Bombs start using Blizzard (FFB) or Flamestrike (FB/TTW). When Hot Streak procs, throw Pyroblasts at random targets. Try not to focus one specific target; spread the threat!


Simplified:
FB/TTW 
Living Bomb > Pyroblast! > Flamestrike > Improved Scorch > Fireball

FFB 
Living Bomb > Pyroblast! > Blizzard > Flamestrike > Improved Scorch > Frostfire Bolt

Cooldown Use


Combustion
Increases your critical strike bonus with fire spells, and also increases critical chance by 10% every stack until 3 non-periodic critical strikes are made. Use whenever because it's great. It's a good lead in to a boss, especially after 5 seconds when you should have trinket, ring, and weapon procs.

Mirror Image
Summons three clones of the caster that deal frostbolt and fire blast damage. Good for a little extra DPS, don't be afraid to use it.

Ice Block
Puts the caster in an un-damagable ice block. Great for dropping threat and saving your life from high boss damage.

Invisibility
Makes the user invisible and drops threat against the user. It takes a little while to work but it can be used effectively when you're about to pull threat OR the group is dying and you want to save yourself.

Mana Shield
Absorption shield that uses mana to absorb incoming attacks. Don't use this all the time, but keep it in mind for heavy damage periods, provided you have enough mana to sustain it.

Evocation
Gain 60% of your total mana over the channel period. This has a decently long cooldown for fire (4+ minutes), so it's best saved in case you go OOM mid fight. Lazy, and well geared mages can use it between fights instead of drinking the literally free mana cakes they make...

Icy Veins (FFB)
20% spell haste and 100% reduction in knock-back for 20 seconds. Invaluable to the Frostfire build, it's almost a mini Bloodlust/Heroism.

Focus Magic (FB/TTW)
While technically a buff, I've included it here so FB/TTW users don't feel left out. Apply it to the spellcaster/healer you think will crit the most. It will, essentially, give both of you a 3% increased crit chance. Arcane and other FB/TTW mages should be given priority because you can effectively trade Focus Magic buffs.


But when do I use...


...Polymorph?
When you're asked to, basically. A good rule of thumb is that if a mob is marked by anything other than Skull or Cross, something should be done to CC it. Also, you may want to use it automatically on any party member that has been mind controlled.

...Spellsteal?
Oh my god. There's something you can spell steal? Quick, use it before an ignorant Shaman or Priest dispells the effect. They just don't understand the joys of having an exotic buff. (So basically use whenever)

...Counterspell?
Whenever you can basically. Unless there is an interrupt order, feel free to counterspell as immediately as you can.

...Arcane Brilliance?
Before the first boss pull and before future boss pulls after anyone dies. It doesn't matter what class they are, they're made smarter by you. (Yes, even Warriors)

...Conjure Refreshment?
Before any boss pull whenever someone requests refreshments. It's only mana and arcane powder, right? It'll grow back!

Gearing

Damage

Hit cap is super important so get that. After you're hit capped, you want crit, above all things. Crit is what you crave. Crit will solve all of your problems. Not enough damage? Crit. Running out of mana? Crit. Too much fireball/frostfire spam? Crit. Literally everything in this build, regardless of how minute it may seem, is affected by crit and the more of it you have, the better the damage, and the higher your mana regen will be. Beyond this, haste and sprit. Don't focus haste or spirit, and don't fear people judging you for having spirit on your gear. Hey! Some mage tiers have spirit, it's useful to us. I mean, 70% of our spirit is turned into crit, right? What you should avoid is gear with raw mp5 on it. That's not for you.


Simplified:
FB/TTW 
Hit rating (446) > Crit > Haste > Spirit

FFB 
Hit rating (368) > Crit > Haste > Spirit

Itemization:

What you'll find is that mages are kind of limited in what they can or can't equip. What you'll want preferably is a one handed sword or dagger and a decent offhand item. Barring this, find a good staff. One hand/Off hand items at the same ilvl of a staff, will be more beneficial spellpower wise.

Wow, was that the first time I mentioned spellpower? It's like, super important, but also meaningless. Every item you equip, barring trinkets, should have spellpower on it. Every cloth piece for sure, and properly itemized rings and necklaces will have spellpower on them. If it doesn't have spellpower, don't take it.


Gemming:

I typically follow the socket requirements because "free stats", but you could do 2x Purified Dreadstone and stack the rest with Runed Cardinal Ruby if you so desire. But... you know... free stats!
