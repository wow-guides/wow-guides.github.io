Introduction

Welcome to the brave new world of Death Knight tanking, my friend. It's a scary world, but with this guide you should be able to conquer it with relative ease. Death Knights are versatile tanks; both able to mitigate physical damage normally, and mitigate magical damage via talents and spells. This makes the Death Knight one of the most utilized tanks for magic heavy fights.

The main draw back to Death Knight tanks is the lack of a shield, however, by choosing blood, you allow youself to quickly heal any damage taken before the healer even notices it's missing. Through reflexive healing, we can make up what we lose by lacking the ability to block.

Others who wish to maximize threat may put remaining points into the Blood or Unholy trees. Blood tanks concerned with magic damage may put points into [Spell Deflection]. There's a great number of ways to build depending on how you wish to tank.

Single Target

For single target threat generation, the most important spell is likely Icy Touch. Icy Touch develops absolutely bonkers amounts of threat, something like 6x it's damage. On the server I play on, a single Icy Touch can net me about 20-30k threat. Beyond that, make sure you apply blood plague with Plague Strike and keep both of your blood runes on cooldown using Heart Strike, Rune Tap, or Blood Tap. If you're not having threat issues, use frost and unholy runes to Death Strike after being hit. The diseases must be on the target for Death Strike to work, so bear that in mind.


Simplified:
DS > HS > Diseases > IT > PS > RS > DC

Multi Target

For multi-target pulls Death and Decay becomes your bread and butter. Drop it under the offending pile of mobs and run in. Icy touch and Plague Strike the priority target and then use Pestilence to spread your diseases. After that quickly cycle targets, lobbing death coils or rune strikes at mobs whose aggro you may be in danger of losing. For the second cycle, use Blood Boil, Heart Strike, Death Strike, or whatever else you wish to maintain aggro.


Simplified:
DnD > Diseases > DS > BB/HS > RS > DC

Cooldown Use


Icebound Fortitude
This is your largest source of damage reduction. Use this as an "oh shit button" when you notice your health dropping rapidly, your healer is out of mana, or your healer is completely lost. It will save your life.
Vampiric Blood
This will increase your healing taken. Since the cooldown is short, use this whenever you think it will be helpful. High damage phases, add phases, beginning of the fight, etc. This will also consume a blood rune.
Anti-Magic Shell
Use this to help endure periods of high magic damage, or prevent a spell based debuff effect from being applied to you. Its use is very subjective and depends largely on the fight mechanics.
Blood Tap
This is a free death rune and will remove one of your blood runes meaning you only need to get rid of the other for Blade Barrier. With 4pc T10 tank gear, it'll offer an additional 12% damage reduction for 10 seconds when used, turning it into another damage reduction cooldown.
Rune Tap
This will heal you for 20% your health. With the glyph of Rune Tap, you will also heal your party. Use this skill as a blood rune after being dealt a heavy strike to negate the need for healing.
Lichborne
This will turn you undead for 10 seconds, negating fear, charm, and sleep effects. As a bonus, if you target yourself and use death coil the death coils will heal you. Can be used as a great way to negate fight mechanics or as an "oh shit" self-heal.
Raise Dead
This will summon a tiny ghoul guardian to fight with you.
Death Pact
This will consume your ghoul guardian, giving you 40% your maximum health. Use this as a life saving "oh shit" self-heal in conjuction with raise dead.
Mark of Blood
This puts a mark on an enemy, causing them to heal whomever they hit each time they deal damage. Best utilized on quick foes, or foes with a large aoes. When used properly, mark of blood can provide both single target and raid healing.
Hysteria
Increases physical damage but also damages the player it's applied to. Should be applied to highest physical damage dps OR Death Knight tank if they are having threat issues.
Empower Rune Weapon
Immediately refreshes all of your runes. Best used either as an "oh shit" to refresh runes for Death Strike, Rune Tap, or Vampiric Blood usage. Can also be used early in the fight to ensure high threat.

Gearing

Threat:

If you worry about your threat, the best thing you can do is get hit and expertise capped. Not that it's as imporant with a Death Knight, since we don't lose our runes when we miss, it's still the best way you can ensure you have high threat. The expertise cap is 26 for dodge and 56 exprtise for parry. Although you'll be tanking, the dodge cap is enough. Hit cap is 8%.


Simplified:
263 hit rating > 200 Expertise rating > *

Defense:

Most importantly, above all else, is to get defense capped. If you're not at the cap, there is a chance that raid bosses will critically strike you, dealing bonkers amounts of damage to you and scaring the healers. To prevent this you MUST reach a defense of 540. Beyond this, you'll be looking for stamina primarily followed by parry and dodge. Dodge rating will result in slightly better returns than parry, however, as both have a diminising return it's best not to go too hard in a single direction.


Simplified:
540 Defense > Stamina > Dodge = Parry > Defense Rating*
* = Beyond 540 defense

Itemization:

With this build you will want to play with any slow (3.5 speed +) two-handed weapon. Ideally a two-handed sword, mace, or axe, with strength and stamina on it. Agility/Stam polearms can be used, albiet at a loss of threat and parry. The runeforge on the weapon should either be Stoneskin Gargoyle or Swordshattering at the player's discretion. Stoneskin Gargoyle will net more stamina and thus always come out above Swordshattering even if the defense cap is already met through gear.

Beyond weapon, every single item on your character should be a plate tanking item. These items should have some combination of Strength, Stamina, Bonus Armor, Defense, Dodge, Parry, Hit, and Expertise. You will never see Armor Penetration, Crit, or Haste on tank armor, jewelery, or trinkets. Unless you are extremely experienced and know the risks associated with running dps and pvp gear, please stick to tanking gear only.

Introduction

Hello friend! Thank you for choosing Unholy, possibly the most under-utilized but highest damage death knight spec on the server. If you can work around all of the bugs and problems that is.

Unholy focuses on maximizing disease and shadow damage, while still doing incredible melee damage with a two-handed weapon. It offers raid utility in a 13% damage increase to any targets affected by blood plague and thus this spec is a caster's best friend.

Build

Talent:

There's little variation to the possible talent for Unholy. Most have settled on a solitary spec that forgoes points in Reaping to place them in Endless Witner

Single Target

For single target damage, you want to focus on keeping diseases up, refreshing them with pestillence, and scourge striking away. That's basically all there is to it.


Simplified:

Your opener should be:

PS -> IT -> SS -> BS -> BS -> RP Dump (Gargoyle or DC)

After that, your continued rotation should look like:

SS -> SS -> BS -> Pest -> RP Dump

You can substitute Pest for BS in the first rotation if you're certian you can hit Pest again before diseases fall off


Multi Target

Here's the fun thing. For multi target dps, just spread your diseases to everything and stand back. Wandering Plague will basically do all of your damage, so keep diseases refreshed and use Blood Boil and DnD for filler. Scourge Strike if you have nothing else to do with frost and unholy runes.


Simplified:
Diseases > DnD > BB > SS > RP Dump

Cooldown Use


Amry of the Dead
Use either just before or as you're running into combat. It's chaotic and fun, but not always necessary.
Summon Gargoyle
Use ASAP, either as your first runic power dump or your second. Does a fair bit of dps, quit helpful before the fight normalises.
Anti-Magic Shell
Use when heavy spell damage is incoming. Generates quite a bit of RP and may save your life.
Raise Dead
Should be used immediately upon switching to this spec. Technically a cooldown but Unholy is a hell of a lot weaker without the permanent pet so treat it more as a buff.
Death Pact
Use as your about to die and in no other instances. You will do less damage without a pet, but dead dps do no dps.

Gearing

Stats:

First and foremost, get expertise and hit capped. The expertise cap is 26 for dodge and the hit cap is 8%. After that, crit,


Simplified:
263 hit rating > 200 Expertise rating > Crit >= Haste > Armor Penetration

Itemization:

With this build you will want to play with any slow (3.5 speed +) two-handed weapon. Always a two-handed sword, mace, or axe, with strength and stamina on it.

Beyond weapon, every single item on your character should be a plate DPS item. These items should have some combination of Strength, Stamina, Critical, Haste, Armor Penetration, Hit, and Expertise. You will never see Dodge, Parry, or Defense on dps armor, jewelery, or trinkets. Try to avoid leather gear with Agility on it. Death Knigts do not nearly benefit enough from agility to make agi gear a viable upgrade, even if it has better secondary stats or a higher item level.
