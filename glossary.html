Glossary of Terms:

Primary Stats:

Strength
Increases attack power and parry. Scales better for plate users (Paladin, Death Knight, Warrior) and for feral tank.
Agility
Increases critical strike, armor, and dodge. For mail and leather classes, it always increases attack power.
Stamina
Increases health pool.
Intellect
Increases mana pool and spell critical chance.
Spirit
Increases both health regeneration and mana regeneration. Favoured regen stat for Priests and Druids.
Armor
Decreases physical damage taken.

Secondary Stats:

Critical Strike Rating
Increases your chance to critically strike, doing increased damage for an attack. Most DPS specs will have a critical bonus for certain types of attacks so critical strikes can do anywhere between 50% and 100% increased damage.
Haste Rating
Increases the speed at which you cast or attack. Some specs benefit from this way more than others.
Armor Penetration Rating
Decreases the efect of enemy armor, meaning that your physical damage will increase. This stat is exponential for specs relying on heavy physical damage. The more ArPen you have, the higher the stat weight is until 100% is achieved.
Hit Rating
Increases your chance to hit against mobs or other players. Melee specs will often have a soft hit cap of 8% while for ranged specs it varies between 11% and 17%. Note: Melee hit and spell hit scale differently, so check the one applicable to your spec.
Expertise Rating
Decreses the likelyhood of enemies dodging or parrying attacks. The Dodge cap on expertise is 26. For melee dps, this is what you aim for.
Defense Rating
Decreases the likilihood of being critically hit by mobs. Decreases the likilihood of being hit by mobs. Increases Dodge, Parry, and Block% slightly. The soft cap for defense is 540, no more, no less. Many guides erroneously report this as 550 for 535 but they're completely wrong. Read this page if you're not a believer in he 540 defense cap, or are curious how the mecanics work.
Dodge Rating
Increases dodge change. This has diminishing returns so don't invest too heavily.
Parry Rating
Increases parry change. This has diminishing returns so don't invest too heavily. Scales lower than dodge but has the added benefit of Parry Haste, which reduces the defender's next melee swing by 40%.
Block Rating
Increases block chance for shield users.
Block Value
Increases the damage mitigated when blocking an attack.
Mana Regeneration (Mp5)
Increases the raw mana renegeration. This is the preferred regeneration stat for shaman and paladins.

Roles:

Tank
This is the individual in charge of getting smacked around by the boss. Often also leads the encounter if there are any movements.
DPS (Damage Per Second)
These are individuals charged with doing damage to the boss. They make up most of the raid and are also responsible for the most wipes.
Healers
This infallible little group of lovelies is charged with keeping the raid alive. Often underapperciated, healers are the backbone of the entire raid and carry a lot of the dead weight around on their shoulders.

Raidspeak:

Boss
This is the central fixture of the encounter. Often denoted by the Skull where a level should be.
Adds
Additional monsters that are spawned during encounters.
Trash
Monsters found between encounters.
Crowd Control (CC)
The act of putting a control spell on a monster or mind controlled player so do not interfere with the encoutner or can be saved until higher priority targets are killed.
Area of Effect (AOE)
Using spells that deal smaller damage per hit to a larger number of mobs in order to clear these mobs en masse.
Peel
The act of dps pulling stray mobs off of healers or squishy ranged dps.
Buff/Debuff
Buffs are helpful spells, you apply them to your friends in the raid group. Debuffs are harmfull spells that bosses or adds put on raid members during an encounter.
Clease
Cleansing is the act of removing a debuff with a debuff removal spell. There are four categories that can be removed: Magic, Curse, Poison, Disease. Not all debuffs can be cleansed.
Tactics
This is the group's game plan for the boss as explained by a member in the group. Often they will describe how certain roles handle certain situations so pay very mindful attention to your role.
Wipe
The result of a failed encounter.
Loot
Spoils of victory or treasure from an encounter.

Furthermore, these rankings are based on the 8 most straightforward bosses in ICC. Certain builds may perform better on the less "tank and spank; single target" style encounters in raids. Therefore take the data here with a grain of salt, and don't send me emails being like "I'm a Survival Hunter and I was top dps on Dreamwalker."

Analysis:

The DPS chart serves to prove what was wrong with Wrath of the Lich King: reliance Armor Penetration pushed physical builds to the forefront. All but Arms, Blood, and Subtlety sit within 20% of the highest possible dps, with two physical builds occupying the top 3. After the debacle that was diseaseless Blood Death Knight, Blood was nerfed into the ground. The mechanics for Arms and Subtlety were never conducive to raiding, relying heavily on burst damage and control rather than sustained damage. Where those builds failed, Fury, Marksmanship, and Combat rogue excelled. Even Kitty druid, with its hybrid bleed and physical damage, made good use of Armor Penetration. Overall though, the issues with balancing Armor Penetration led Blizzard to remove the stat with patch 4.0.1.

Arpen aside, this patch was very well balanced. 16 dps builds are within 25% of Fury Warrior. All of these builds have a purpose, had a role in a raid group, and could put down some decent numbers. The ones that fell below 75% are mainly pvp focused specs.

Analysis:

Compared to the DPS averages, the HPS chart is less useful for one simple reason: tank healers will always have higher HPS because their targets are taking the brunt of the damage. Tanks could very well see 80% of the incoming damage in an encounter. This is why both Discipline and Holy Paladin are showing abnormally high HPS on this chart. That said, the balance for the raid healing specs is spot on if you're comparing them. Even though Holy Priest is shown doing 71% of the HPS of Holy Paladins, it has 92% of the HPS of Resto Druid which fulfills a similar role in the raid. The gap between the "tank healing" specializations is much higher, some of which can be accounted for by Discipline Priest's reliance on untracked absorption effects.

Food for Thought:

As with all things, your personal experience may differ from the data. It's very likely that you're playing on a private server with its own balance changes, bugs, and mechanics. Because of this, you may find you can do more damage and more healing than that listed above.

The ranking data shown here is collated from World of Logs. If you click on that link and look on ICC25HC rankings, you will see it's consistent with the relative damage and healing posted above.

Again, approach this with a grain of salt but do not discount its validity. This chart should only apply to the most blizzlike of servers, however, if you're on a server that claims to be blizzlike and your experience is wildly different, perhaps you should ask the staff some hard questions. Furthermore, the data above is an average across several bosses, specifically straightforward bosses with few interuptions. Certain builds may excel at some fights versus others, it is simply just a matter of mechanics.
